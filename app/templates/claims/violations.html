Violations

Select a violation category below to begin a report. Add a report for each type of violation you have experienced.Note: Don't see your issues here? More are coming! Also, send us a note. We will track the law and add a report category.

Why file multiple reports?

Ex Parte

Off-record, lawyer-only sessions, excluded from trials or hearings, TOPs and OSCs granted immediately without notice, etc.
Abuse of Temporary Orders

TOPs and OSCs removing significant rights for months or even years, without evidentiary hearing or trial, based on frivilous claims, blatant perjury, etc.
Excessive Restrictions

Supervised visits or zero contact order without cause, restricted to a county or city bounds, threatened with contempt for unrealistic orders, etc.
Judicial Ethics

Judge repeat appoints AFCs, and others; does not report appointments, remains on case with known conflict of interest(s), orders legal fees without financial review, etc.
Discrimination

Discriminatory statements, decisions that are provide an unfair advantage to one party, one party has a lawyer the other does not, etc.
Right to Counsel

Custodial rights removed without being provided a lawyer, poor person requests are ignored or denied without financial review, one party is high wealth and other party has 18B, etc.
Administrative Violations

Person is not given or is denied access to the record, court records are altered or intentionally false, the courtroom does not notify you of purpose of appearances, the assignments of "experts" and AFCs are not filed, etc.
Procedural and Timeline

Proceedings exceed 6 months, decisions are not placed in writing within 60 days, trial is conducted without any evidence exchanged, rights are removed without trial, etc.
Insufficient Evidence

Your custodial rights, or housing are removed without evidence that meets unfit parent standards, evidence of abuse and domestic violence is ignored or obstructed from the record, requests to see evidence are ignored or even denied, you are denied the right to send subponeas, .
Why File Multiple Reports?
  • Documenting all the violations you encounter in a single report enables offenders to hide the scale of what is happening. By submitting them as unique reports, each claim must be considered by administrative bodies.
  • Submitting what you can finish in one sitting, and adding more reports later helps manage trauma and PTSD.
  • Submitting violations by type supports collective action and better enables us to track patterns of abuses.
  • Creating a report as the violations occur builds a timely record; you can choose to publish or file later when you feel ready.
