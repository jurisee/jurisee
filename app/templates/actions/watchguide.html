Court Watch Guide

When court watching, it’s important to be taken seriously. Court Watchers must be respectful and polite at all times. Here are some tips that can help.

  1. Dress appropriately. Dress shirt and slacks or a skirt are best. Try to avoid jeans.
  2. Take notes. Notes on plain paper are acceptable for legal purposes (should that be helpful for that single case).
  3. However, to alert others and to ensure there is a verifiable, independent record that can also be used to support collective action, please be sure to complete the Court Watch Form.
  4. When completing the Court Watch Form, provide at least the name of the judge, and the names of any other involved persons from the court that you can identify (e.g., AFC, evaluator, and the lawyers).
  5. If you’re familiar with courtroom procedures, note any laws or regulations that are ignored. Pay attention to discriminatory behaviors by the lawyers and judge.
  6. By law, courts open to the public.There must be a written order to close the court room. This includes family courts and custody cases. A court watcher should always ask to see the written order that has closed the court room if they are ordered out.
  7. Above all, present a dignified, silent presence in the courtroom. Court watchers need to be taken seriously. This means no “making faces”, chewing gum, or other inappropriate actions. If you must communicate to a court watcher sitting nearby, do so by writing notes, NOT whispering. (courtesy of Families Against Court Travesties)
  8. You may want to bring a sweater in case the courtroom is cold.
